## 3. WHERE GOLD & SILVER ADD MOST VALUE
- **High-Inflation & Currency Debasement Regimes:** As seen in Turkey, metals are a primary tool for preserving purchasing power when a currency is losing its value. They are a direct and liquid store of wealth outside the control of any single government or central bank.
- **Periods of Systemic Financial Risk:** While stocks and real estate depend on a functioning credit system and stable economic conditions, gold and silver have no counterparty risk. They provide a foundational layer of security for a portfolio when the stability of the broader financial system is in question.
- **Geopolitical Instability:** The reports note that geopolitical tensions drive safe-haven demand. Metals are universally accepted assets that tend to appreciate during times of international conflict or uncertainty, acting as a portfolio stabilizer when other assets may falter.
- **Hedging Against the Unknown:** Metals are not a bet on a single outcome but insurance against a range of negative possibilities—from persistent inflation to a credit crisis or a sudden loss of confidence in financial assets.

## 4. ACKNOWLEDGED LIMITATIONS
- **Lack of Yield:** Unlike rental properties or dividend-paying stocks, physical gold and silver do not generate an income stream. Their value is realized through capital appreciation and, most importantly, capital preservation.
- **Limited Participation in Productive Growth:** Metals do not directly capture the value created by technological innovation or economic expansion in the way that equities can. A company like ASML grows by creating new technology; gold's value is constant and serves as a measure for that growth.

## 5. ALIGNMENT WITH OTHER PERSPECTIVES
- **Complementing Real Estate:** Real estate offers utility (shelter) and potential cash flow, which are valuable attributes. Gold and silver complement this by offering liquid, portable, and globally recognized wealth preservation that is not tied to a specific location or its associated risks.
- **Balancing Equities:** Stocks provide exposure to human ingenuity and economic growth. A prudent portfolio can utilize equities for growth potential while holding a core position in precious metals as a non-correlated anchor, providing stability and insurance against the very systemic risks that can derail financial markets.
